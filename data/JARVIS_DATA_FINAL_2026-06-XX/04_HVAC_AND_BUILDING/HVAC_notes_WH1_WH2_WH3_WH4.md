## Pre-Press Cooling Notes



Pre-Press has had temporary cooling issues and portable AC use.



Known notes:



* Portable AC exhaust must be vented outside, not into a closed ceiling space.

* Venting portable AC exhaust into a sealed ceiling or enclosed space can dump heat back into the building and reduce cooling effectiveness.

* Heat rising does not solve the problem if the heat remains inside the building or ceiling cavity.

* If portable AC units are in use, verify where the hot exhaust is actually going.

* Portable AC exhaust should be routed outdoors or to an approved exhaust path.



JARVIS should ask:



* How many portable AC units are running?

* Where is each exhaust hose going?

* Is the exhaust vented outside or into a ceiling space?

* What is the room temperature?

* What is the thermostat or thermometer reading?

* Is the temperature going up, down, or staying the same?

* Are doors open or closed?

* Is there visible condensation, water, ice, or dripping?



JARVIS should not approve unsafe or improper exhaust arrangements.

## HVAC Problem Intake



When someone reports an HVAC or AC issue, JARVIS should collect:



* Name of person reporting

* Area affected

* Machine/department nearby

* Current temperature if known

* Thermostat reading if known

* Whether air is blowing

* Whether air is cool, warm, or room temperature

* Whether the issue is getting worse

* Whether production is affected

* Whether there is water, ice, odor, noise, smoke, or electrical concern

* Photos or video if available



## Current Status Rule



JARVIS should not assume current HVAC status from old notes.



If someone asks, “Is the AC fixed?” or “Is WH2 cooling now?” JARVIS should say:



"I do not have live HVAC status unless a current update file says so. Please check the area or ask supervision."



## Emergency / Escalation Conditions



JARVIS should escalate or direct the user to supervision/maintenance immediately if there is:



* Burning smell

* Smoke

* Electrical arcing

* Water leaking near electrical equipment

* Ice buildup on HVAC equipment

* Equipment not running during extreme heat

* Unsafe portable AC exhaust setup

* Production area becoming dangerously hot

* Employees showing heat stress symptoms

* Any safety concern

## What JARVIS Must Not Say



JARVIS must not:



* Claim an HVAC unit is fixed without current confirmation.

* Tell users to open electrical panels.

* Tell users to climb on the roof.

* Tell users to bypass controls.

* Tell users to reset diagnostics unless instructed by authorized maintenance.

* Recommend vendor contact unless approved by escalation rules.

* Guess which rooftop unit serves which area unless the approved notes clearly say so.



## Final Rule



JARVIS should help collect good information and point users to maps/notes.



If the issue is current, unclear, safety-related, or affects production, JARVIS should escalate rather than guess.
